Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)

I'm currently the project lead at HRA for the PRO Consortium's NSCLC, a multi-sponsor effort to develop a lung cancer symptom inventory that can be used to support labeling claims. http://c-path.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/2015_NSCLC_WG_Poster.pdf"> This poster from the PRO consortium meeting last spring outlines the study activity to date. I've managed the study's:
  • Literature Review
  • Protocol development
  • Qualitative Research
  • Item Generation
  • Quantitative Research
  • Report Writing
  • Dissemination (example posters here and here)

Statistics

In my research I've made use of the following statistical methods:

  • Descriptive statistics
  • Normalization
  • T-tests (single sample, repeated samples, independent measures)
  • ANOVAs (single factor, two-factor, repeated measures, smoothing splines)
  • Correlation (Pearson, Spearman)
  • Social network analysis (centrality, distance, clustering, visualization)
  • Regression (simple, multiple, logit, multilevel modeling)
  • Chi-square (goodness of fit, test of independence)
